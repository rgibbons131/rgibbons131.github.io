Overview

Purpose

How often do you want to watch a show to kill some time, but you have no idea what to watch? What about a website with a database of shows and movies from various genres? You would be able to be as specific or vague as you want. The website would generate a show for you to watch. With a simple sign in system, you would be able to keep track of all the shows you have seen too, meaning you wouldn't get repeat suggestions.

Audience

My audience is your general college student. So many of us have time on our hands when we can't hang out with friends, we are out of youtube videos to watch, and we've just finished the show we were binging. This site will be a sleek, easy to navigate place where you can quickly go to find new shows to watch.

Content

Home page

Watchlist aims to be an easy-to-use site where you can drop in when you're bored and find a new show to watch. I want to cater to a large demographic. In totality, this idea would take connection to a large database of various shows and movies. It would be an ideal partnership to be able to partner with IMDB.
The main page will have a few key pieces of information on it. It will explain how the information about shows is collected. Hopefully I would be able to say that the source is from IMDB, or the international movie database, showing that the information found is credible and extensive.
There will also be a summary of bingeability as seen bellow. This will help other people understand the collective nature of the site as well as giving them understanding of this as a tool to help make their decision.
The last major piece of information that would be advertised on the main page is that the site includes which streaming service the show or movie are currently on.

Images for the Home page

Gallery/list

Because it is fairly strait forward, I am going to make an extra page that is the gallery/selector of the shows. This in theory would be the main page that people would be interacting with. Here they would be able to set filters such as genre, medium, length, and even bingeabilty scores or critic ratings, but most importantly streaming services. Instead of going through each of their services individually, they'll be able to look at their whole catalogs automatically.

Images for the Page 2

Show summaries

For the purpose of the website plan, I will create a page that exemplifies the explanations of shows. A few things that will be in each description will be a summary of the show or movie.
This initial description would ideally be directly copied from IMDB, as their database is quite extensive in total content, accuracy, and specificity. It would also include a few ratings from at least two sources, and then a public poll on “bingeabilty.”
Also, on each show page there would need to be at least one picture from the show advertisements, maybe a trailer shot or a poster that was used to promote it. There would need to be a trailer whenever possible. The most important piece of information that I would like to include on the page is what streaming services provide access to the show or movie. This will be one of the big selling points.

Images for the Page 3

Bingeability

My second page will be largely about the bingeability of the shows. There will be a poll completed whenever you select that you finished a show. This show will initially have two options, the first will be a 1-10 rating of how good the show was to binge, and the second item is an opt in to a more detailed survey.
The detailed survey will contain some opinion ratings such as filmography, character development, humor, story-telling, and maybe a few other items to be determined at a later date. I want someone to be able to look at the bingeabillity scores and judge for themselves what type of a show they are getting themselves into.
I think this would also benefit people who like more niche or fringe tastes, because they can pick the opposite of what the public generally thinks. While that may seem small, I have known lots of people that hate the general populations approved romances or who hate societies collective sense of humor. It is a very easy way to tailor to those people while not having to design the site around them.
